# Basic Experiment Design

[Threshold-based Flow Funding](https://hackmd.io/@linuxiscool/BysrT2d3Jl) is a potentially transformative framework that arose from the MycoFi movement. We’re excited about this vision and want to test it live. 

Each person in the network defines a **minimum floor** (what they need to do meaningful work) and a **maximum ceiling** (beyond which money automatically flows outward). The network self-distributes based on everyone’s forward flow allocations. 

## Proposal

- 3-6-month pilot
- 20-100 people (from 2-4 core communities / networks)
- $300K - $1M+ needed in philanthropic seed funding to start (~$3000 min income for experiment duration for everyone)

## Guiding Question(s)

- Does threshold-based flow funding produce more equitable distribution than a network's social topology / initial resource distribution predicts? (Does this system eventually honor a community-led signal about value, or does it just dress up moneypower dynamics in different clothes?)
- Does a network's collective allocation preference converge toward something coherent — or just reflect whoever set their thresholds highest? (link to community pool support contribution as well as seeing if subnet flows become insular)
- (MAYBE LONGER TERM/ second experiment?) When the whole network is under resource stress, do allocation preferences shift toward collective need — or toward personal security? (Hypothesis might be that a network designed around explicit minimum guarantees will show *more* redistributive behavior under stress, not less, because the min floors removes the fears that drives hoarding…)

### **What else this experiment could answer - which should we focus on? Others?**

- Can someone hold multiple threshold accounts across different networks?
- Can overflow fund skill gaps before those people are even in the network?
- When community priorities and positional power diverge — does the system surface it?
- Does a skills matrix reduce the popularity problem for membrane entry?
- What's the half-life of the seed capital under live conditions? How can ‘investors’ see what their funding does?
- what is healthy upstream and downstream visibility?
- What initial variables MUST have nobs to adjust for this experiment?

# Simple Rules

All apply to all holons. Holons can be: 

- people
- sub-nets (experiments / projects)
- networks
- anything else needed?

## **Structural Rules - don't change (or, if these are changed, it’s a new experiment):**

1. Holons all the way down - Same rules apply to every node
2. Creation of a non-human holon must meet minimum viable membrane definition (and stewardship?) requirements
3. Thresholds track total *inflow per month* — not current account balance (ungameable)
    - is this reasonable? would this req ZK stuff?
4. Everyone's minimum is fully funded for 6 months before they join
5. Your maximum is never exceeded — overflow routes outward automatically
6. Membrane only opens to new people when the pool can cover 6 months of their minimum

## **Working Rules (adjustable by consent) - maybe at individual Network or Subnet levels?**

- Starting min: **$3,000/month** — starting max: **$8,000/month** (uniform for first 90 days)
- First **$500/month** of overflow goes into the community pool before you direct the rest anywhere
- Above that — flow your surplus to whoever/whatever you want, no approval needed
- Thresholds can change **once per quarter** max, with 30 days notice
- If the experiment continues / morphs longer than 6 m… If **30%+ of participants are below minimum** in any month → membrane closes, community conversation within 14 days
    - articulate cell explosion and cell drying out
    - tie skill functions to new member list order
    - …
- New people join based on a **skills/functions matrix** the founding cohort defines in month 0 — not just who people know

## What we're NOT doing in v1 experiement

- No financial returns promised — purely philanthropic
- No automated fund transfers — manual entry is deliberate
- No reputation scores or rankings
- No governance voting workflow
- No punishment or exit mechanisms (observe first)
