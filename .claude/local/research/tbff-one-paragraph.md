Threshold-Based Flow Funding is a novel resource allocation
mechanism that addresses sustainable funding challenges in
decentralized networks by establishing minimum viability
thresholds for participants while preventing resource
concentration through controlled overflow redistribution. The
mechanism works by first ensuring that all participants
receive their minimum sustainable funding levels (if
possible), then distributing remaining funds up to maximum
thresholds, after which any excess "overflows" are
redistributed through the network according to participants'
allocation preferences - creating a recursive flow where
funds can move through multiple hops before finding their
optimal destination. This approach combines the wisdom of
crowds seen in quadratic funding with practical
sustainability guarantees, creating a self-regulating
economic system inspired by natural water flows where
resources automatically flow from areas of excess to areas of
need, ultimately balancing individual contributor
sustainability with efficient network-level allocation while
leveraging the collective intelligence of participants to
determine where resources can create the most value.
